Back Squat
Phase 2 replaces trap bar with back squat for max quad and glute recruitment. Bar on mid-traps, chest tall, knees track toes. This is the PL standard for peak strength testing at weeks 5–8. Go heavy — 80–85% of 1RM.

Hinge from the hips, bar close to legs, feel hamstring lengthening under load. 3-sec lowering phase. Heavy RDLs are the single biggest hamstring strength driver — used by every PL physio to close the strength gap between quads and hamstrings.

Box at knee height, dumbbells in hands. Drive through the heel of the working leg — don't push off the floor foot. Builds unilateral leg power that directly transfers to cutting and sprinting off one leg.

Trap Bar Jump (Light)
30% of your deadlift max. Squat down, then explode up leaving the ground. This is the bridge between strength and power — trains rate-of-force development. Used in PL academies right after heavy squats for contrast training effect.

Nordic Hamstring Curl
Every session, every phase. 4-sec lowering. Non-negotiable injury prevention. If you drop this when training gets intense, that's exactly when hamstrings tear.

Speed Development
Max velocity · Sprint technique · Top-end speed
⚡ WARM-UP (15 min)
Sprint Mechanics Drills
A-skips × 4 × 20m → B-skips × 4 × 20m → straight-leg bounds × 3 × 20m → build-up strides 60/70/80/90/95%. This is the PL standard pre-speed session warm-up. Never rush it — CNS needs to be firing before you sprint at 95%+.
15 min protocol
⚡ MAX VELOCITY WORK (30 min)
Flying 40s
30m rolling start, then max sprint for 40m. Fly through the zone — don't accelerate in it. This isolates true top speed. Full 4-min rest between reps. PL GPS data shows wingers hit 32–35 km/h in matches — this is how you get there.
6 × 40m (4 min rest)
Wicket Runs
Small hurdles or cones spaced at your natural stride length. Overstriding is one of the biggest speed limiters — wickets force optimal stride frequency and length simultaneously. 4 runs through, full recovery.
4 × through course
Resisted Sprint → Free Sprint
Use a resistance band or sled — 20m resisted, then immediately 20m free. The overspeed effect after resistance makes the free sprint feel effortless and teaches your legs to move faster. Manchester City and Arsenal both use this protocol.
6 × 20+20m
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Hamstring + Calf + Hip Flexor Stretch
Lying hamstring pull 90 sec each → downward dog calf stretch 60 sec each → half-kneeling hip flexor 90 sec each. These three structures absorb the most stress at max sprint speed.

Alternate sole taps on top of ball — as fast as possible while keeping control. Builds fast-twitch foot coordination. Time yourself: target 60 taps in 20 seconds. PL academy benchmark for U18 attackers.

MOVING CONTROL (15 min)
Cone Weave — Inside Only
6 cones, 1m apart. Use only the inside of the foot through the weave. Forces a compact, controlled touch with the strongest surface of your foot. Both feet, 10 passes each.
10 × each foot
Cone Weave — Outside Only
Same setup, outside of the foot only. The outside touch is the most used touch in beating a player 1v1. Most players neglect it in training. Do as many reps on your weaker foot as your stronger.
10 × each foot
Free Dribble — Speed Changes
Dribble freely in 15m × 15m space. Every 5 sec, either explode to max speed for 2 sec or slow to walking pace. Trains the rhythm of a real match — speed change is what beats defenders, not pure pace.
8 min
02
1v1 Skill Moves
Scissors · Cruyff · Step-over · Elastico
FOUNDATION MOVES (20 min)
Double Scissors
Swing one foot around the ball, then the other, then push away with the outside of the second foot. The double scissors is one of the most commonly used moves in PL wide play. Practice slowly first — the sell is in the body shape, not the feet. 20 slow, 20 game-speed.
40 reps each side
Cruyff Turn
Shape to pass or cross, drag the ball behind your standing foot and turn 180 degrees. The most useful skill move for a wide midfielder under pressure near the byline. Master it both ways — you'll need the left-foot version when closed down from your right.
30 reps each side
Matthews / Benitez Cut
Shape outside with the outside of the foot, cut back inside with the inside. The oldest and most effective 1v1 move in football. Simple, deceptive, unstoppable when timed right. 100 reps total is not too many — this should be automatic.
50 reps each foot
ADVANCED MOVES (20 min)
Elastico (Flip-Flap)
Push the ball outside with the outside of the foot, immediately flick it back inside with the inside of the same foot in one motion. Ronaldinho's signature. Hardest move in this drill set — start from standing, slow. Once consistent, add a 5m approach run.
20 reps each foot
Roulette (Zidane Turn)
Drag ball back with one sole, spin 360 while dragging with the other sole, accelerate out. Used by Zidane constantly under pressure in tight spaces. Builds spatial awareness and confidence on the ball when pressed. Slow is smooth, smooth is fast.
20 reps each direction
String-Together Combo
Pick 3 moves from this session and do them back to back without stopping the ball — scissors into Cruyff into Matthews cut, for example. PL wide players practice combining moves because real defenders adjust. End every ball work session with 5 min of free combination play.
5 min free combo
03
First Touch & Receiving
Cushion touch · Direction · Aerial control
WALL WORK (20 min)
One-Touch Passing Wall — Vary Surface
Pass against a wall, control the return one-touch with inside, outside, sole, and laces alternating. The PL standard: a winger receives the ball from every angle with every surface. No look-down touches. Head up every rep.
10 min continuous
Directional First Touch
Pass at the wall, but set your touch 2m to one side as if turning away from pressure. Every touch should set you up to move — not just control the ball where you stand. Alternate: touch left, touch right, touch behind. This is what separates good from elite.
10 min directional
AERIAL CONTROL (15 min)
Throw-to-Self Chest & Thigh Control
Throw ball up, control with chest down to thigh down to foot — keep it alive without it touching the ground more than once. Aerial control is a massive differentiator for wingers receiving long diagonal balls. 20 reps each surface.
20 reps each surface
Juggling — Weak Foot Only
No strong foot allowed. The goal is not a high juggle count — it's developing touch and feel on the weaker foot under no pressure, so it becomes instinctive under pressure. 3 sets until failure, record your best count each session.
3 × max reps
Juggling — Combinations (Head/Thigh/Foot)
Keep ball alive using head, thigh, and foot in sequence. Trains the switch between surfaces mid-rally — exactly what happens when you control a clearance from a corner kick. 5 min continuous attempt.
5 min
04
Passing & Crossing
Weight of pass · Driven cross · Whipped ball
PASSING PATTERNS (20 min)
Short Pass Accuracy — Wall or Target
10m passes, aim for a 30cm target square. Alternate feet every rep. Weight and accuracy together — a misweighted pass is as bad as a missed one. PL midfielders target 95%+ short pass accuracy in training. Track yours.
60 passes each foot
Driven Pass — 25–30m
Strike through the centre of the ball with the laces, follow through low. Driven passes at 25–30m with pace and accuracy are essential for switching play as a wide midfielder. 20 reps each foot. Count how many reach the target with pace.
20 reps each foot
CROSSING (20 min)
Whipped Near-Post Cross
Approach at pace from wide, open your body, whip the ball with the instep toward the near post at head height. The near-post cross is the most dangerous delivery in PL wide play — harder to defend than a far-post ball. Aim for the same landing zone every rep.
15 reps each foot
Cut-Back Cross (Byline)
Take the ball to the byline, cut back hard across the 6-yard box. The cut-back is statistically the highest-value delivery in PL — more goals per cross than any other type. Roll it along the ground, pace into the zone. Right and left.
15 reps each foot
Early Cross — Before the Byline
Cross from 10–15m out before the defender can close. Strike with the inside of the foot, flight the ball into the far-post area at 2m height. This is the Trent Alexander-Arnold delivery — one touch set, one touch cross. Practice the weight and arc.
15 reps each foot
05
Shooting & Finishing
Driven shot · Placement · First-time finishing
TECHNIQUE (15 min)
Driven Laces Shot — Standing
Ball stationary, standing start. Lock ankle, strike through the centre-top of the ball with laces, follow through toward target. 10 reps right, 10 reps left. Focus on a clean strike — no shin, no toe. Record any that hit your target corner.
20 reps each foot
Placed Inside Foot — Far Corner
Pick a corner, pass the ball into it with inside of the foot at pace. Placement beats power at every level. Thierry Henry, Mane, and Salah all scored the majority of their goals with placed inside-foot shots. Accuracy is everything here.
20 reps each foot
MATCH FINISHING (20 min)
Moving Shot — Receive and Shoot
Roll a ball to yourself 5m, take one touch to set, shoot first-time. Your shooting technique must hold up when you're moving — if you only practice from standing you'll get the yips in matches. 30 reps, alternate which direction the ball comes from.
30 reps moving
Cut Inside and Shoot
Start wide right, take 3 dribbling touches, cut inside onto your left foot and strike. This is the defining attacking pattern for a right-footed right winger or inverted left midfielder. 15 reps from each side. Time the run to simulate coming off a defender.
15 reps each side
Weak Foot Finishing
Finish all remaining shots with your weaker foot only. 20 reps, mix of placed and driven. A player who can finish with both feet doubles the number of shooting angles available to them. Marcus Rashford, Leroy Sane, Bukayo Saka — all spent hours on their weaker foot. So should you.
20 reps weak foot
06
Pressing Angles & Defensive Duels
Press triggers · Jockeying · Interception reads
PRESSING MECHANICS (20 min)
Angled Press Sprint — Force to One Side
Set up two cones 10m away at 30 and 60 degrees. Sprint to the 30-degree cone to cut off the inside pass lane — force the opponent outside. PL pressing is always angled, never straight. 10 reps, alternate the angle.
10 × angled sprint
Shadow Jockey Drill
Face a partner (or a cone you imagine is moving) and stay side-on between them and goal. Low centre of gravity, weight on the back foot, small quick steps. Jockeying forces errors — don't lunge. PL fullbacks who jockey well win more duels than those who dive in.
6 × 15 sec bouts
DUEL SIMULATION (15 min)
Body Shape Shielding
Ball at your feet, lean your body against a wall or post to simulate a defender. Hold the shield position for 5 sec while keeping control, then turn away. Trains the core tension and low-body positioning needed to shield under physical pressure.
15 × 5 sec holds
Interception Read — Cone Gates
Set 4 cone gates in a grid. Pass the ball through one gate, sprint to cut off the return path through another. Trains reading passing lanes and anticipating where the ball will go — a skill PL defenders develop through thousands of repetitions, not instinct.
10 min grid work
1v1 Defending — Turn and Track
Start beside an attacker (cone). Ball played in — attacker tries to get past, you turn and stay between them and the goal for 5 sec. No lunging. Win the duel by position, not by gambling. The best defenders in the PL win duels before contact, not during it.
10 × 5 sec duels

ASSIGNED BREAKDOWNS Players whose game most maps to yours
Trent Alexander-Arnold
RB / Right Midfielder — Liverpool & England
Why study him: Defines the modern wide defender role. Elite delivery from deep, reads pressing triggers before others, uses body shape to open passing angles. His off-ball positioning is what enables his on-ball output.
Watch how he scans before receiving — never faces play blind
Study his press-avoidance body shape when pressed by a winger
Observe the curved run to cross early before the block
Note how he recovers defensively when caught high — sprint angle and cover shadow
Joao Cancelo
RB / LB / Inverted fullback — Barcelona, Bayern, Man City
Why study him: The blueprint for the inverted fullback. Moves into midfield when team has the ball, creates overloads, and uses pace to recover. If you play as a wide midfielder or high fullback, his movement patterns are essential viewing.
Watch his infield movement when the winger pushes wide
Study how he creates passing triangles by positioning between lines
Observe his 1v1 defending stance — always half-turned to see ball and runner
Note his recovery sprint path — always inside track to cut off the goal side
Achraf Hakimi
RB / Wing-back — PSG & Morocco
Why study him: The most explosive wide defender in the world. His acceleration, timing of overlap runs, and 1v1 defending at pace directly mirror the physical profile you're building. Study him specifically for how he transitions from sprint back to defensive shape.
Observe his overlap timing — he goes early, not when the ball arrives
Study his deceleration into 1v1 defending — drop of hips, patient feet
Watch how he uses his body to channel attackers wide
Note his set piece positioning — near post vs far post reads
Bukayo Saka
Right Winger / Right Midfielder — Arsenal & England
Why study him for defence: Saka is elite at defensive transitions — the hardest skill for attacking wide players to develop. Watch how he tracks back, selects when to press vs. drop, and how his defensive work rate enables his attacking freedom.
Watch his press trigger decisions — when does he go, when does he hold?
Study his body shape when defending 1v1 on the right — cuts off the inside
Observe how quickly he transitions from final third to defensive shape
Note his movement patterns when Arsenal are out of possession — shadow covers

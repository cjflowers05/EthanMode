Weeks 1–4
Premier League Athletic Base
Modeled after PL academy strength & conditioning: build your aerobic engine, reinforce hamstring and knee resilience, establish sprint mechanics, and develop the full-body athleticism that underpins every elite defender-winger.
1
Lower Strength + Acceleration
Force production · Sprint mechanics · Hamstring armor
⚡ ACTIVATION (10 min)
Hip Flexor + Glute Activation
Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch → banded clamshells → glute bridges. Wake up the posterior chain before loading it.
2 × 10 each
🏃 SPRINT MECHANICS (15 min)
A-Skip + B-Skip Drills
High knee drive, dorsiflexed foot, land under hips. PL S&C staple — trains sprint mechanics before sprint fatigue sets in.
4 × 20m each
Falling Start 10m Sprints
Lean forward until you must run — trains first-step acceleration. Full recovery between reps. Used at Liverpool and Chelsea academies.
8 × 10m
🏋️ STRENGTH (30 min)
Trap Bar Deadlift
Brace core, push the floor away — don't pull the bar up. 2-sec descent. This is the single best total-body strength builder for field athletes. PL clubs use it specifically because it loads the hips without spinal compression.
5 × 5 @ 75–80%
Bulgarian Split Squat
Rear foot elevated, front shin vertical. Single-leg strength is critical for deceleration and cutting. 3-sec descent, explode up.
4 × 8 each leg
Nordic Hamstring Curl
The #1 PL hamstring injury prevention exercise. Lower as slowly as possible — 4–5 sec — use hands only to break the fall. Studies show 51% reduction in hamstring tears with consistent use.
4 × 5 (eccentric)
Depth Drop → Box Jump
Step off a low box, land softly, immediately explode onto a higher box. Trains reactive strength — the ability to absorb and redirect force fast.
4 × 4 reps
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Supine Hip + Hamstring Stretch
90/90 hip stretch → lying hamstring pull → piriformis stretch. Hold each 45 sec per side. Don't skip this — it's when adaptation happens.
45 sec × 3
2
Agility + Aerobic Engine
Change of direction · Reaction · Match conditioning
⚡ ACTIVATION (8 min)
Dynamic Warm-Up Circuit
Leg swings (front/side) → walking lunges → high knees → lateral shuffles → 3 build-up strides at 70–80% max.
1 × circuit
🔀 AGILITY (20 min)
5-10-5 Shuttle (Pro Agility)
Start centre, sprint 5 yards right, plant and sprint 10 yards left, sprint back 5 yards to centre. Low centre of gravity on the plant. Used in every PL pre-season testing battery.
6 × reps (90s rest)
T-Drill
Sprint 10 yd, shuffle left 5 yd, shuffle right 10 yd, shuffle back 5 yd, backpedal to start. Tests lateral quickness and change-of-direction mechanics.
6 × reps (90s rest)
Reactive Mirror Drill
Face a partner or wall — react and mirror lateral movement. Trains first-step reaction, not just planned agility. 10-sec bursts, full recovery.
8 × 10 sec
❤️ AEROBIC BASE (25 min)
Yo-Yo Intermittent Recovery Test (Level 1)
20m shuttle runs at progressively increasing speeds with 10-sec recovery jogs. The gold standard PL aerobic capacity test. Aim to beat your previous distance each session.
20–25 min
🧘 COOL-DOWN (8 min)
Quad + IT Band + Calf Stretch
Standing quad pull → lateral lunge IT band → wall calf stretch. All 45 sec per side. These three tighten most in defender-wingers.
45 sec × 3
3
Upper Body + Core Power
Pushing · Pulling · Rotational core · Shoulder resilience
⚡ ACTIVATION (8 min)
Band Pull-Aparts + Scapular Push-Ups
Wake up the rotator cuff and scapular stabilisers before pressing. PL physios use this to bulletproof shoulders against aerial challenges and falls.
3 × 15 each
🏋️ STRENGTH (35 min)
Push Press
Slight knee dip, drive the bar overhead explosively — it's a whole-body lift. Trains the same muscle chain used when shielding, jumping, and winning headers. 3-sec lower.
4 × 5 @ 70%
Weighted Pull-Ups
Full hang to chin over bar. Add a dumbbell between feet or a belt if bodyweight is easy. Lat and upper-back strength is critical for physical duels and aerial wins.
4 × 6–8
Dumbbell Row (3-point support)
Chest on incline bench, row to hip — don't rotate. Unilateral back strength that directly translates to contesting the ball with your non-dominant shoulder.
4 × 10 each
🌀 ROTATIONAL CORE (15 min)
Cable/Band Rotational Chop
High-to-low and low-to-high. Rotate from the hips, not the arms. This builds the twisting power that makes your cross and defensive clearance explosive.
3 × 12 each side
Pallof Press
Anti-rotation — resist the cable pulling you sideways. Core must stabilise while arms press and pull. Trains exactly the core stiffness you need when shielding the ball.
3 × 12 each side
Copenhagen Plank
Side plank with top leg on a bench — groin and adductor load. One of the most important injury-prevention exercises for footballers. Build up from 10 to 30 seconds per side.
3 × 20 sec each
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Thoracic Mobility + Pec Stretch
Foam roll thoracic spine (10 reps) → door frame pec stretch 45s × 2 → child's pose lat stretch 45s. Opens up the chest and mid-back that tighten during pressing days.
10 min flow
4
Active Recovery + Full Mobility
Zone 2 cardio · Deep stretch · Tissue quality
🚴 ZONE 2 CARDIO (30 min)
Easy Bike or Jog
Stay at 60–70% max HR — you should be able to hold a conversation. This is not a rest day; it's an aerobic engine builder. PL clubs use Zone 2 specifically on the day after heavy lifting to flush lactate and keep the aerobic base growing.
30 min
🧘 FULL MOBILITY FLOW (30 min)
World's Greatest Stretch
Lunge forward, drop elbow to ground, rotate arm to sky. The single movement that hits hip flexor, groin, thoracic rotation, and ankle mobility simultaneously. Used in virtually every PL pre-training warm-up.
5 reps × 3 each side
90/90 Hip Mobility Drill
Sit in 90/90 position (both hips at 90°), rotate forward over front leg then backward. Unlocks internal and external hip rotation — a known injury-risk area for wingers making sharp cuts.
10 reps each side
Couch Stretch
Rear foot on wall/couch behind you, upright torso. The deepest hip flexor stretch available. 2 min per side minimum — this is where defenders lose mobility from sitting and driving.
2 min × 2 each side
Foam Roll: Quads, Hamstrings, IT Band, Calves
Slow rolls — 30 sec per area. Pause on tight spots. This is tissue quality maintenance. PL clubs have players do this daily during season; in pre-season it's every recovery session.
30 sec × 4 areas
Ankle CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)
Seated, slow full-circle ankle rotations — biggest range you can control. Ankle stiffness is a hidden performance limiter for wingers. 5 slow circles each direction per foot.
5 circles × 2 each
5
Speed Endurance + Match Conditioning
High-speed running · Lactate tolerance · Transition speed
⚡ WARM-UP (12 min)
Progressive Build-Up Strides
4 × 80m strides at 60%, 70%, 80%, 90% of max. Last stride should feel like almost-sprinting. This primes the CNS before max-intensity running.
4 × 80m
⚡ SPEED ENDURANCE (30 min)
Flying 30s
20m rolling start into 30m at 95–100% max. Trains top-end speed, not just acceleration. Full 3-min recovery between reps — this is quality work, not fitness work.
6 × 30m (3 min rest)
Repeat Sprint Protocol
6 × 40m at 90%+ with only 20-sec rest between. This simulates the repeated sprint demands of a winger in a PL match — builds lactate tolerance and recovery speed.
6 × 40m (20s rest)
Defensive Transition Shuttles
Sprint 25m forward, decelerate, immediately sprint 25m back. Mimics the attack-to-defence transition. Train deceleration mechanics — most hamstring injuries occur here, not at top speed.
8 × 25m × 2-way
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Walking + Hamstring + Hip Flexor Stretch
5 min easy walk to drop HR → 2 min hamstring stretch each leg → 1 min hip flexor stretch each side. Never skip cool-down after max sprinting — this is your injury insurance.
10 min
6
Power + Agility Finisher
Plyometrics · Ladder work · Reactive agility · Weekly finisher
⚡ ACTIVATION (8 min)
Jump Rope + Lateral Band Walks
2 min jump rope → 2 × 15 lateral band walks each direction. Primes the ankle stiffness and hip abductors needed for plyometric work.
8 min circuit
💥 PLYOMETRICS (20 min)
Broad Jumps
Swing arms, jump as far forward as possible, land soft with bent knees. Measures and builds horizontal power — the same explosive force behind your first-step acceleration.
4 × 5 reps
Single-Leg Bounds
Hop on one leg for distance, landing same foot. Develops unilateral explosive power and ankle stiffness. PL wingers need this for cutting at pace off one leg without losing speed.
4 × 6 each leg
Lateral Bounds (Skater Jumps)
Push off one leg laterally, stick the landing on the other. Builds the lateral explosive power and deceleration control you use every time you cut past or recover against a winger.
4 × 8 each side
🪜 AGILITY LADDER (15 min)
Ladder: Ickey Shuffle + In-In-Out-Out
These are the two patterns that most directly transfer to soccer footwork. Focus on rhythm and quiet feet — don't bang the ladder. 3 passes each pattern, rest 45s.
3 × each pattern
Ladder → Sprint Out
Complete one ladder length then sprint hard for 15m off the end. Trains transitioning from footwork to full-speed running — exactly what happens when you beat a player and burst away.
6 × with 15m sprint
🧘 WEEK WRAP (10 min)
Full Lower Body Stretch Flow
Hip flexor → hamstring → quad → groin → glute → calf. 45 sec each, both sides. End of week — take time with this. Your body rebuilt itself this week; now flush it out.
45 sec × 6 areas
Weeks 5–8
Power & Speed
The base is built. Now you load it. This phase maxes out explosiveness, sprint velocity, and the ability to repeat high-speed efforts — the exact qualities that separate PL wingers from everyone else.
1
Max Strength Lower
Heavy compound lifts · Posterior chain · Explosive finishing
⚡ ACTIVATION (8 min)
Glute Bridge ISO Hold + Hip Flexor Mob
3 × 30-sec glute bridge hold — squeeze hard at the top. Then 90-sec hip flexor stretch each side. You're about to load heavy; these two prep the exact muscles that absorb force under the bar.
3 × 30 sec
🏋️ STRENGTH (40 min)
Back Squat
Phase 2 replaces trap bar with back squat for max quad and glute recruitment. Bar on mid-traps, chest tall, knees track toes. This is the PL standard for peak strength testing at weeks 5–8. Go heavy — 80–85% of 1RM.
5 × 3 @ 82%
Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
Hinge from the hips, bar close to legs, feel hamstring lengthening under load. 3-sec lowering phase. Heavy RDLs are the single biggest hamstring strength driver — used by every PL physio to close the strength gap between quads and hamstrings.
4 × 6 @ 75%
Weighted Step-Ups
Box at knee height, dumbbells in hands. Drive through the heel of the working leg — don't push off the floor foot. Builds unilateral leg power that directly transfers to cutting and sprinting off one leg.
4 × 8 each leg
Trap Bar Jump (Light)
30% of your deadlift max. Squat down, then explode up leaving the ground. This is the bridge between strength and power — trains rate-of-force development. Used in PL academies right after heavy squats for contrast training effect.
4 × 5 @ 30%
Nordic Hamstring Curl
Every session, every phase. 4-sec lowering. Non-negotiable injury prevention. If you drop this when training gets intense, that's exactly when hamstrings tear.
3 × 6 (eccentric)
🧘 COOL-DOWN (8 min)
Quad + Hip Flexor + Glute Stretch
Standing quad → couch stretch 90 sec each → figure-4 glute stretch 45 sec each. These three shorten most under heavy squat loads.
90 sec each
2
Speed Development
Max velocity · Sprint technique · Top-end speed
⚡ WARM-UP (15 min)
Sprint Mechanics Drills
A-skips × 4 × 20m → B-skips × 4 × 20m → straight-leg bounds × 3 × 20m → build-up strides 60/70/80/90/95%. This is the PL standard pre-speed session warm-up. Never rush it — CNS needs to be firing before you sprint at 95%+.
15 min protocol
⚡ MAX VELOCITY WORK (30 min)
Flying 40s
30m rolling start, then max sprint for 40m. Fly through the zone — don't accelerate in it. This isolates true top speed. Full 4-min rest between reps. PL GPS data shows wingers hit 32–35 km/h in matches — this is how you get there.
6 × 40m (4 min rest)
Wicket Runs
Small hurdles or cones spaced at your natural stride length. Overstriding is one of the biggest speed limiters — wickets force optimal stride frequency and length simultaneously. 4 runs through, full recovery.
4 × through course
Resisted Sprint → Free Sprint
Use a resistance band or sled — 20m resisted, then immediately 20m free. The overspeed effect after resistance makes the free sprint feel effortless and teaches your legs to move faster. Manchester City and Arsenal both use this protocol.
6 × 20+20m
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Hamstring + Calf + Hip Flexor Stretch
Lying hamstring pull 90 sec each → downward dog calf stretch 60 sec each → half-kneeling hip flexor 90 sec each. These three structures absorb the most stress at max sprint speed.
90 sec each area
3
Upper Power + Core
Explosive pressing · Med ball power · Anti-rotation strength
⚡ ACTIVATION (8 min)
Band Pull-Aparts + Cuban Press
Rotator cuff and scapular prep. 3 × 15 pull-aparts, 3 × 10 Cuban press with light band or weight. Don't skip — shoulder injuries in aerial challenges are common and preventable with 8 min of this.
3 × 15 / 3 × 10
💥 POWER (25 min)
Bench Press (Explosive Intent)
Lower controlled 2 sec, then press as explosively as possible even if the bar doesn't move fast. Rate-of-force development matters more than the weight here. 75% of 1RM.
5 × 4 @ 75%
Med Ball Chest Pass (Wall)
Stand 1m from wall, explosive chest pass as hard as possible, catch and reload fast. Trains the same explosive push mechanics as hand-offs in physical duels. 3–5kg ball.
4 × 10 reps
Weighted Pull-Ups (Max Strength)
Add 10–20kg if bodyweight is easy. 3-sec descent. Pulling strength is often undertrained in footballers — it directly helps you hold off defenders and win headers.
4 × 5 heavy
Med Ball Rotational Throw (Wall)
Stand side-on to wall, rotate and throw hard — catch and repeat without pause. This is the exact rotational power pattern behind your cross, your tackle, and your body-to-body challenges.
4 × 8 each side
🌀 CORE POWER (15 min)
Ab Wheel Rollout
From knees to start. Roll as far as you can control with a rigid core, return slowly. The anterior core strength built here transfers directly to sprint posture and deceleration mechanics.
4 × 8 reps
Hanging Knee Raise
Dead hang, pull knees to chest with control — don't swing. Progress to toes-to-bar as you get stronger. Hip flexor strength in long movement ranges is critical for kicking power and sprint mechanics.
3 × 12 reps
🧘 COOL-DOWN (8 min)
Thoracic + Pec + Lat Stretch
Foam roll thoracic 10 passes → doorframe pec stretch 60 sec each → child's pose lat stretch 60 sec. Your upper back will be tight after all that pressing and pulling.
8 min flow
4
Active Recovery + Mobility
Zone 2 · Deep tissue · Movement quality
🚴 ZONE 2 (25 min)
Bike or Light Jog
60–70% max HR, conversational pace. Phase 2 is your highest-load block — this session exists to keep the aerobic system ticking without adding stress. Don't turn it into a workout.
25 min easy
🧘 DEEP MOBILITY (35 min)
World's Greatest Stretch + Spiderman Lunge
Alternating: WGS → Spiderman with overhead reach. Move slow and breathe into the stretch. 5 each side. This combination hits every hip and thoracic restriction point simultaneously.
5 reps × 3 each
Pigeon Pose (Deep Glute)
The most effective glute and hip external rotator stretch. 2 min each side minimum. Heavy squatting compresses these structures — this undoes it. Don't rush through it.
2 min × 2 each side
Full-Body Foam Roll
Quads → IT band → hamstrings → glutes → thoracic spine → calves. 30–40 sec each, pausing on anything tight. In Phase 2 this is your tissue maintenance — the heavier you lift, the more you need this.
6 areas × 40 sec
5
Repeated Speed + Change of Direction
Sprint repeats · COD under fatigue · Lactate tolerance
⚡ WARM-UP (12 min)
Dynamic Mobility + Build-Up Strides
Leg swings, lateral lunges, hip circles, then 5 × build-up strides (60→95%). Never skip this before COD work — most ankle and knee injuries happen in the first 15 min of a session when tissue is cold.
12 min protocol
⚡ SPEED + COD (35 min)
505 Agility Test Reps
Sprint 15m, plant and turn 180° at a cone, sprint back 5m. Tests and trains the ability to brake and redirect under speed. Time yourself — PL wingers typically hit under 2.0 sec. Track your improvement weekly.
8 × each leg (90s rest)
3-Cone L-Drill
3 cones in an L shape. Sprint to cone 1, back, around cone 2, figure-8 around cone 3. Used in NFL combine but adopted in PL pre-seasons for its COD specificity. Drives the lateral deceleration and replant mechanics you use every time you track a run.
6 × each direction
Repeat Sprint with Short Rest
8 × 30m at 90%+ with only 15-sec rest. This is brutal by design. You're training the ability to sprint again immediately after sprinting — exactly the repeated demand of a winger chasing back then breaking forward. Hold your form through every rep.
8 × 30m (15s rest)
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Walk + Stretch Protocol
5-min walk to flush lactate, then hamstring → hip flexor → quad → calf, 60 sec each side. After repeated sprints this is essential — lactate pooling in the legs without a proper flush causes next-day stiffness that kills Day 6.
10 min total
6
Full Power Finisher
Plyometrics · Contrast training · Weekly peak output
⚡ ACTIVATION (10 min)
Jump Rope Complex
2 min easy → 1 min single-leg alternating → 30-sec double-unders attempt. Primes ankle stiffness and fast-twitch recruitment before plyometric work. The CNS needs this before maximal jumps.
3.5 min circuit
💥 CONTRAST PLYOMETRICS (30 min)
Squat → Squat Jump (Contrast Pair)
3 heavy goblet squats immediately followed by 3 maximal squat jumps. The post-activation potentiation effect from the heavy squat makes the jumps more explosive. Rest 2.5 min between sets.
4 sets (3+3 contrast)
Depth Drop → Max Vertical
Step off a 40–50cm box, land and immediately jump as high as possible. Ground contact should be under 0.2 sec — fast and stiff. Trains reactive strength and tendon elasticity. The goal is the shortest ground contact, not the biggest drop.
4 × 4 reps (2 min rest)
Max Broad Jump → Sprint
3 consecutive broad jumps for max distance, immediately sprint 20m from landing. Trains horizontal power then transitions it directly into running speed. Used by PL academies to bridge gym power into field speed.
4 × 3 jumps + 20m
🧘 WEEK WRAP (12 min)
Full-Body Stretch + Breathing
Hip flexor → hamstring → glute → quad → thoracic → shoulder. 60 sec each. End with 10 deep slow breaths — diaphragmatic breathing after peak training sessions drops cortisol and accelerates recovery. Week 5–8 is the hardest block. You made it through another one.
60 sec × 6 areas
Weeks 9–12
Match Fitness & Sharpness
You're strong, you're fast — now make it match-specific. Reduce gym volume, maximise intensity, and peak everything for game day. This is how PL players taper into a season while staying sharp.
1
Match Prep Strength
Maintain strength · Reduced volume · High quality
⚡ ACTIVATION (8 min)
Glute + Hip Flexor + Hamstring Activation
Same as always — clamshells, glute bridges, half-kneeling stretch. In Phase 3 you're maintaining what you've built, not breaking new ground. Quality reps over quantity in every session.
2 × 12 each
🏋️ STRENGTH — REDUCED VOLUME (30 min)
Trap Bar Deadlift
Back to trap bar — lower spinal stress for in-season maintenance. Keep weight at 80–85% but drop volume to 3 sets. Enough stimulus to maintain strength, not so much that it impacts match readiness.
3 × 4 @ 82%
Bulgarian Split Squat
3 sets only — maintain the strength asymmetry balance you've built. Still heavy, still slow descent. Single-leg strength is actually more important in-season when match loads accumulate on one side.
3 × 6 each leg
Nordic Hamstring Curl
The most important exercise to keep in Phase 3. Match sprint loads are highest in this phase — hamstring resilience is everything. 3 sets, 5 reps, 4-sec eccentric. Every session, no excuses.
3 × 5 (4-sec ecc)
Single-Leg Box Jump
One leg at a time — step off, land on same leg. Keeps the reactive power system primed without the volume fatigue of Phase 2. 4 reps per leg is enough stimulus.
3 × 4 each leg
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Lower Body Stretch Flow
Hamstring → hip flexor → quad → glute. 60 sec each. Longer holds in Phase 3 — you're managing accumulated fatigue from 8 weeks of hard training.
60 sec × 4 areas
2
Match Sharpness Session
Game-speed agility · Short sprints · Reactive decision speed
⚡ WARM-UP (12 min)
Full Dynamic Warm-Up + Strides
Dynamic mobility → high knees → carioca → shuffle → 5 build-up strides. You've done this all 12 weeks — your body knows this sequence now. Get through it with intent, not routine.
12 min
⚡ GAME-SPEED WORK (30 min)
Reaction Start Sprints (Visual Cue)
Set up a training partner or use coloured cones — react to a signal, sprint 15m. Trains true reactive speed, not just timed starts. PL players spend significant Phase 3 time on reactive patterns because match speed is always triggered by a stimulus.
10 × 15m reactive
Short COD Circuit: 5-5-5
3 cones, 5m apart. Sprint 5m, cut, sprint 5m back, cut, sprint 5m. Max effort each rep, 45-sec rest. These match the exact distance and direction-change patterns that dominate PL position data for defenders and wide players.
10 × circuit (45s rest)
Transition Sprint: Press → Recovery
Sprint 25m pressing forward, slow to jog 10m, immediately sprint 25m tracking back. Mimics the exact energy demand pattern of a defensive winger through a 90-minute match. 6 reps, 2-min rest.
6 × full pattern
🧘 COOL-DOWN (10 min)
Hamstring + IT Band + Calf Stretch
90 sec each structure per leg. At week 9+ your soft tissue is under cumulative load — longer holds matter more now than at week 1.
90 sec × 3 each

Upper Maintenance + Core
Maintain upper strength · Core power · Shoulder health

Band Pull-Aparts + Scapular Activation

Push Press

3 × superset
🌀 CORE (15 min)
Dead Bug
Lower back flat to floor, extend opposite arm and leg slowly without arching. The purest test of anterior core stability. This is what holds your sprint posture together at 85 min when you're exhausted.

Copenhagen Plank
30-sec holds now — you've built from 10 sec in Phase 1. Groin and adductor strength is at its most critical in Phase 3 when match loads are highest.
3 × 30 sec each
Pallof Press (Heavy Band)
Heaviest band you can control. Anti-rotation core stiffness is what makes you impossible to knock off the ball. Finish Phase 3 stronger here than you started Phase 1.

Full Recovery Day
Zone 2 · Extended mobility · Tissue work · Mental reset
🚴 ZONE 2 (20 min)
Easy Bike or Walk
Shorter than Phase 1 and 2 recovery sessions — you want blood flow without any accumulated fatigue going into the peak days. 60% max HR maximum. This is active recovery, not training.
20 min easy
🧘 EXTENDED MOBILITY (40 min)
Full Hip Complex
90/90 → pigeon → couch stretch → frog stretch. 2 min each position per side. In Phase 3, hip mobility directly affects sprint mechanics and injury risk. This is the longest mobility session of the 12 weeks by design.
2 min × 4 positions
Full-Body Foam Roll + Lacrosse Ball
Foam roll major muscle groups, then use a lacrosse ball on glutes, calves, and foot arch. These smaller tools get into areas the roller can't. At week 9+ your tissue needs this level of attention.
40 sec × 8 areas
Diaphragmatic Breathing Protocol
Lie flat, hand on belly. Inhale 4 sec through nose (belly rises), hold 2 sec, exhale 6 sec through mouth. 10 rounds. PL clubs use this in Phase 3 specifically for nervous system recovery and mental clarity before peak weeks.
10 × 4-2-6 breath

Peak Match Conditioning
High-speed running volume · Match demands · Tactical fitness
⚡ WARM-UP (12 min)
Progressive Mobility + Strides
Full dynamic warm-up, then 6 × build-up strides finishing at 95%. This session will hit the highest training load of the entire 12 weeks — the warm-up has to be thorough.
12 min thorough
⚡ MATCH CONDITIONING (40 min)
High-Speed Running Intervals
Run at 85–90% max speed for 30 sec, jog 30 sec recovery. This mirrors PL GPS data: a winger averages 8–12 high-speed running bouts per half, each 15–30 sec, with brief recovery. You're training the exact energy system the game demands.
12 × 30 sec on/off
Match Sprint Simulation
Sprint 35m, walk 15m, sprint 35m, walk back. Repeat without full rest. Mimics the sprint-recovery pattern of a PL winger in the 60–80 minute period when the game is most intense and fatigue is highest.
8 × 35m double
Anaerobic Power Finisher
5 × 10-sec all-out sprint with 50-sec rest. Pure anaerobic power — the energy system behind your decisive burst in the final 10 minutes of a match when others slow down. These 5 reps should hurt.
5 × 10 sec max

Full Lower Body Stretch + Breathing
